#  Aviation Accident Analysis Dashboard

##  Project Overview

This project presents an interactive **Aviation Accident Analysis Dashboard** designed to explore historical aviation accident data and uncover key safety insights. The dashboard enables stakeholders to monitor accident trends, injury severity, airport risk hotspots, aircraft damage levels, and accident phases to support aviation safety improvements.

---

##  Objectives

* Analyze total aviation accidents and injury severity distribution
* Identify top accident-prone airport locations
* Track yearly accident trends
* Examine aircraft damage categories
* Understand accident causes by flight purpose and flight phase

##  Dashboard Features

### 1. Top 10 Accident-Prone Airports

Displays airports with the highest number of reported aviation accidents.

### 2. Accident Trend by Year

Line chart showing yearly fluctuations in aviation accident frequency.

### 3. Aircraft Damage Distribution

Pie chart illustrating aircraft damage levels:

* Destroyed
* Substantial
* Minor
* Unknown

### 4. Accidents by Purpose of Flight

Breakdown of accidents by flight type such as:

* Personal
* Instructional
* Aerial Application
* Business
* Positioning

### 5. Accidents by Phase of Flight

Tree-map showing high-risk phases including:

* Landing
* Takeoff
* Cruise
* Maneuvering
* Approach

##  Insights Generated

* Landing and Takeoff phases account for the highest accident volume.
* Personal and Instructional flights contribute the majority of incidents.
* Anchorage (AK) and Miami (FL) are top accident-prone airports.
* Accident frequency shows a gradual decline over recent years.
